Miss Baker (1957-1984)

Simian American Space Pioneer

Miss Baker and Miss Able were the first primates to travel to space and return safely aboard Jupiter AM-18 on May 28, 1959. Miss Baker was purchased along with 25 other Squirrel Monkeys at a pet shop in Miami, Florida and conducted her training at Cape Canaveral. She was chosen to become an astronaut after trainers deemed her excellent at staying calm and put for 24 hours, a very important skill to survive space flight in her tiny space cabin. Miss Baker enjoyed tremendous fame, was featured on the cover of LIFE magazine and married twice! She famously resided at the U.S. Space & Rocket Center in Huntsville, Alabama with her second husband Norman, entertaining museum visitors and receiving 100–150 letters a day from schoolchildren. Join us in honoring the brave animals who traveled to space at the time when the space program was “not quite there yet”. Every important innovation required the input of bold and visionary individuals.
